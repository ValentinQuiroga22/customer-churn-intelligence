# Findings

## Finding 1 - Overall Churn Rate

Overall churn rate is 26.5%.

Approximately 1 in every 4 customers leaves the company.

---

## Finding 2 - Contract Type

Month-to-month customers show a churn rate of 42.7%.

One-year contracts:
11.3%
  
Two-year contracts:
2.8%

Contract length appears to be one of the strongest retention drivers.

---

## Finding 3 - Customer Tenure

Median tenure:

Retained customers:
38 months

Churned customers:
10 months

Customer retention is especially critical during the first year.

---

## Finding 4 - Highest Risk Segment

Customers with:

- Month-to-month contract
- Less than 9 months tenure

show a churn rate of 52.9%.

This is the highest-risk segment identified during the exploratory analysis.

---

## Finding 5 - Service Adoption

Customers without Online Security and Tech Support show churn rates above 41%.

Customers using these services exhibit significantly lower churn rates.

---

## Finding 6 - Internet Service

Fiber optic customers show a churn rate of 41.9%.

This is substantially higher than DSL customers and requires further investigation.

---

## Finding 7 - Payment Method

Customers using Electronic Check show a churn rate of 45.2%.

This is more than double the churn observed in other payment methods.

Further analysis is required to determine whether this is a direct driver or a correlated behavior.

---

## Finding 8 - Revenue Impact

Customers who churn generate higher monthly revenue on average:

Retained customers:
61.27

Churned customers:
74.44

This indicates that customer churn is disproportionately affecting higher-value customers.
